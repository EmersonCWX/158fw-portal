Vermont ARNG

Army Aviation.
Green Mountain Strong.

The real Vermont Army National Guard Aviation element has served the state and nation for decades, operating utility and light helicopters from the Army Aviation Support Facility in Jericho, Vermont — nestled between the Green Mountains and Lake Champlain.

Vermont ARNG aviators have deployed in support of state emergencies, overseas contingency operations, and national defense missions. We carry that tradition of service and professionalism into Microsoft Flight Simulator.

Organizational Structure

Our Units

The virtual Vermont Army National Guard Aviation element is modeled after the real VTARNG AASF at Jericho, Vermont, operating two primary aircraft platforms.

Headquarters
vVTARNG Aviation Command
📍 AASF Jericho, Vermont

Unit-level command and staff element. Responsible for mission readiness, standards, training publications, and administrative oversight of all assigned crews and personnel.

Medium Utility
UH-60 Black Hawk Element
📍 AASF Jericho, Vermont

The primary medium-lift platform. Crews fly simulated air assault, MEDEVAC, external sling-load, and utility transport missions across Vermont and the Northeast in Microsoft Flight Simulator.

Light Utility
UH-72 Lakota Element
📍 AASF Jericho, Vermont

The light utility and reconnaissance platform. UH-72 crews conduct border patrol simulation, liaison missions, training support, and light logistics ops in MSFS.

Application Process

How to Join

Our process is straightforward. We want to get to know you, not vet your resume.

01
Submit Your Application

Head to the Become a Fighter Pilot page and fill out a short application. Tell us about yourself, your interest in the wing, and what you hope to gain. We read every submission.

02
Introductory Review

Wing leadership reviews your application. If selected, you'll receive an invitation to our Discord server and be introduced to your designated sponsor — a current member who will guide you through onboarding.

03
Onboarding & Orientation

You'll receive access to our training materials, unit publications, and the member portal. An orientation session with your sponsor covers unit culture, rules, and what to expect from the MQT program.

04
Begin MQT Training

Once onboarded, you enter the Multi-crew Qualification Training pipeline alongside an instructor pilot. Progress through stages at your own pace, completing lessons, evaluations, and check rides to earn your wings.

05
Mission Ready

With MQT complete, you're fully mission-qualified and eligible for all unit events, exercises, and operational sorties. Welcome to the Green Mountain Boys.
